Lunar Lander Door Mechanism

Design Process and Validation

Our sponsored team project consisted of designing a novel lunar lander door mechanism. We researched existing designs, brainstormed new ideas given our generated requirements and specifications. We also conducted verification through some engineering analyses. Additionally verification tests were conducted using a small scaled section of our door under a vacuous environment.

Some skills we practiced throughout this project included: research, brainstorming, concept sketching, CAD modeling, prototyping and report writing. Below is our final report and a video showcasing its functionality.

Control System for Linkage

Design of Mechatronics System

For this project we were given a task in a similar manner to the RMP project. The goal for this project was to create a linkage that moves around in a playing field to shine light on various photo sensors As such, this was more involved with linkage/transmission/joint design, engineering analyses and its control system. Additionally more emphasis was placed on report writing and documentation.

In this project we designed our own linkage with the goal of weight saving determined through CAD and its speed under a fixed acceleration profile as well as its deviation in transmission angle from the optimal 90 degrees determined through MSC ADAMS. After, choosing the optimal linkage design through a decision matrix, we created several manufacturing and assembly plans.

After manufacturing and assembling our linkage onto the playing field, we wired our electrical subsytem that involved: a motor, motor driver, microcontroller, proximity sensors and limit switches. We then tuned our proximity sensors and our PID controller to achieve optimal performance.

Below is our final report and a video showcasing its performance.

Robotic Machine Player (RMP)

Design and Manufacturing

This was an introductory project to designing and manufacturing that was "gamified". A summary of the tasks that were involved are: collaborating with other teams to strategize, conceptualizing and brainstorming ideas for a design to complete our task(transporting and delivering blocks), creating functional requirements, evaluating our concept designs against a decision matrix to see which one is best suited for our requirements and finally designing it.

Some tools we got to use throughout the class involved: brainstorming tools such as cards, 3D modeling with Solidworks, engineering analyses using free body diagrams and some mechanics of materials. Additionally, we had the opportunity to create a report and a showcase of our robot as seen below.
